March 2020

On building things that outlast you

There's a particular kind of satisfaction in creating something that still runs correctly three years after you've forgotten how you did it. Not because you were clever, but because you were clear. Clarity, it turns out, is the closest thing to permanence we can achieve as of now.

The same might be true for every act of making: the music that lasts isn't always the most complex, and the buildings that endure aren't always the tallest or the fanciest. It's the ones honest enough to admit what their limitations are. Love set us free, the more love there is in our heart, the more we can create that outlasts.
